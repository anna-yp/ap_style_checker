We’re ready whenever you are—ask about capitalization, numerals, or how to cite a source.
Should I capitalize "governor-elect" when it follows a name in a caption?
Keep it lowercase once the title follows the name: “John Rivera, the governor-elect, said…” Use uppercase only when it directly precedes the name.
Need a quick refresher on numerals for millions.
Pair a figure with the word “million” — “3 million” — unless the sentence begins with the number. Include decimals if precision is needed (e.g., “3.2 million”).
